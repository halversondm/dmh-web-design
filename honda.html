Recently, I restored a 1970 CD175.  The bike had only 2400 miles when I began repairing it.  I had taken some pictures a couple of years ago when I wasn't able to ride the bike (under age).  My father had purchased the bike back in the '70s and never really got to enjoy it.  It sat around for years deteriorating.  When I was a kid the bike used to sit in front of our '69 Corvette and I used to climb on it and pour what ever my mom gave me to drink down it.  The bike was moved to a new town   in 1985 where it began it's first overhaul in 1989.  You see my dad owned an auto parts store with a full machine shop in the back.  So it was very easy to clean and assemble the bike.  The tank needed to be replaced.  I wonder why.   Also the carb needed to be cleaned with solvent.  Amazingly to this point the original battery was in place.  The bike was finished and driven once and not moved since three weeks ago.  My father brought home a new battery and discovered that there were more problems.  The carb and fuel cock were soaked in Chem-Dip.   Right now the toughest part is keeping the tank clean.  Of course the gas which had not been drained out had turned into varnish.  Currently the tank is still a mess, but it does run.  The bike had no other problems except the tank.  I am trying to devise a way to clean the tank with out ruining the paint or any rubber parts.
